Moonshine Sour

Year-Round · Serves 1 · 5 Minutes

Tart, frothy, and dangerously drinkable. The sour that puts Montana corn whiskey center stage.

The foam catches the porch light -- a perfect white pillow floating on pale gold, three drops of bitters drawn into a tiny heart by someone who has had exactly two cocktails.

There is a reason the sour has outlasted almost every other cocktail format. It is balanced. It is forgiving. And when you use a spirit as clean as moonshine, every element gets to shine. The lemon juice brings the snap. The simple syrup rounds the edges. And the egg white -- if you choose to use it -- turns the whole thing into something silky and elegant, the kind of drink that makes people pause mid-sip and ask what you just handed them.

Skip the egg white and you have a bright, punchy sipper that works just as well over ice in a rocks glass. Add it and you have a drink that belongs in a coupe, bitters art and all. Either way, you are building on a template that has been perfected over two centuries. Moonshine just makes it Montana.

Make one for yourself. Then make one for whoever is sitting closest. This drink was meant to be passed around.

Recipe

Moonshine Sour

Prep: 5 min
Serves: 1
Season: Year-round

Ingredients

  • 2 oz moonshine
  • 1 oz lemon juice
  • ¾ oz simple syrup
  • 1 egg white (optional)
  • • Angostura bitters for garnish
  • • Lemon wheel for garnish

Instructions

1

If using egg white, add it to the shaker first with the moonshine, lemon juice, and simple syrup. Dry shake (without ice) vigorously for 15 seconds to emulsify the egg white.

2

Add ice to the shaker and shake hard for another 15 seconds until very cold.

3

Strain into a rocks glass over fresh ice, or into a coupe glass if serving up.

4

Add three drops of Angostura bitters on top of the foam and drag a toothpick through them for a decorative pattern. Garnish with a lemon wheel.

Serve It In

A coupe glass for the full egg-white experience, or a rocks glass over ice for a casual pour. Either pairs well with a Montucky Cold Snack Mug on the side.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the egg white safe to drink raw? +
The risk is very low with fresh, properly refrigerated eggs. If you prefer, use pasteurized egg whites or aquafaba (chickpea liquid) as a vegan alternative -- it foams the same way.
What is a dry shake? +
Shaking without ice. This lets the egg white emulsify and create a thick, stable foam before the ice dilutes and chills the drink. Then you add ice and shake again to get it cold.
Can I skip the egg white? +
Absolutely. Without the egg white you get a brighter, more citrus-forward drink. It is delicious either way -- the egg white just adds a velvety texture and visual appeal.
