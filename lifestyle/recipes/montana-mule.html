Montana Mule

⏲ 3 min 🍴 1 serving 🌠 Year-round

Every good bar in Montana has a version of this drink, and every bartender will tell you theirs is the best. The Montana Mule is the kind of cocktail that settles arguments before they start. It is simple enough that anyone can make it, strong enough that it earns respect, and refreshing enough that one always leads to a second. The copper mug is not optional. It is part of the experience, the way the metal frosts over, the way the cold hits your lip before the drink does.

Picture a Thursday evening in Helena. The workweek is not quite finished, but it is close enough that nobody is pretending to be productive anymore. You are at the bar with people you have known for years, and someone orders a round. The ginger beer fizzes against the moonshine, the lime cuts through everything, and for a few minutes the world narrows to the conversation in front of you and the drink in your hand. The Montana Mule does not try to be complicated. It just tries to be perfect, and it usually succeeds.

Montana Mule

Prep: 3 min Serves: 1 Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 2 oz Montucky Moonshine
  • 4 oz ginger beer
  • 0.5 oz lime juice
  • Lime wedge for garnish
  • Ice

Instructions

  1. Fill a copper mug or rocks glass with ice.
  2. Pour 2 oz Montucky Moonshine over the ice.
  3. Add 0.5 oz fresh lime juice.
  4. Top with 4 oz ginger beer.
  5. Stir briefly and garnish with a lime wedge.

Serve It In

A copper Moscow Mule mug is the classic choice and keeps the drink extra cold. The metal conducts the chill from the ice, so every sip hits crisp and clean. No copper mug? A highball glass works fine.

Complete the Moment

Round out the bar with the Montucky Moonshine Mug — an enamel camp mug that works just as well for coffee at dawn as it does for cocktails at dusk.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between ginger beer and ginger ale?

Ginger beer is brewed and has a much stronger, spicier ginger flavor than ginger ale, which is carbonated water with ginger flavoring. For a proper mule, always use ginger beer. The spice is what makes the drink.

Can I batch this for a party?

Mix the moonshine and lime juice in a pitcher ahead of time. Add the ginger beer just before serving so it stays fizzy. Figure one recipe per person and scale accordingly.

Does the copper mug actually matter?

It keeps the drink colder longer and adds a slight metallic note that ginger beer lovers appreciate. But honestly, the drink tastes great in any glass. Do not let a missing mug stop you from making it.
