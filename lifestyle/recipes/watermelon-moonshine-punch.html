Watermelon Moonshine Punch

Summer · Serves 6 · 15 Minutes

The crowd-pleasing pitcher drink that turns any summer gathering into a proper Montana celebration.

The screen door slaps shut behind you, bare feet on warm pine planks, and someone is already carrying the punch bowl out to the dock.

There is a particular kind of afternoon that only happens at a Montana cabin in July. The lake is glass. The cooler is stocked. Nobody is checking the time. This punch was born for exactly that moment -- the kind of drink you mix in a big bowl with whatever fruit the farm stand had that morning, then ladle into mason jars while kids cannonball off the end of the dock.

Fresh watermelon gives it that candy-pink color and natural sweetness, while the moonshine and triple sec keep things honest. The lemon-lime soda adds just enough fizz to make it dangerously easy to drink. You will want to make a double batch. Trust us -- the first bowl never survives past four o'clock.

This is not a cocktail you overthink. It is a cocktail you share. Set it on the porch railing, drop in a ladle, and let the weekend handle itself.

Recipe

Watermelon Moonshine Punch

Prep: 15 min
Serves: 6
Season: Summer

Ingredients

  • 4 cups cubed watermelon
  • 1 cup moonshine
  • ½ cup triple sec
  • 2 cups lemon-lime soda
  • • Lime slices for garnish

Instructions

1

Blend the cubed watermelon until smooth, then strain through a fine mesh sieve to remove pulp. You should have about 3 cups of juice.

2

In a large punch bowl or pitcher, combine the watermelon juice, moonshine, and triple sec. Stir well and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

3

Just before serving, add the lemon-lime soda and stir gently to combine without losing carbonation.

4

Serve over ice in mason jars or punch cups. Garnish each glass with a lime slice and a small watermelon wedge.

Serve It In

This punch looks best in mason jars or a classic glass pitcher. Grab a Montucky Mason Jar to keep the cabin vibes authentic.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make this ahead of time? +
Yes -- mix everything except the soda up to 4 hours ahead and keep it refrigerated. Add the lemon-lime soda right before serving so it stays fizzy.
What if I can't find fresh watermelon? +
Frozen watermelon chunks work great and double as ice cubes as they thaw. You can also substitute store-bought watermelon juice -- just reduce to 3 cups since it is already liquid.
How strong is this punch? +
With the soda and watermelon juice diluting the spirits, each serving comes out to roughly the strength of a glass of wine. Easy to sip, so pace yourself.
