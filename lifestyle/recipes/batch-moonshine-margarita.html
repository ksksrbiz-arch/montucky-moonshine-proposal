One pitcher. Eight glasses. Zero trips back to the bar. The tailgate margarita that feeds the whole crew.

Batch Moonshine Margarita

⏲ 8 min 🍴 6 servings ☀ Summer

The pitcher is already sweating by the time you set it on the tailgate. Someone reaches for it before you even finish rimming the first glass. That is how you know you made the right drink.

A margarita is only as good as its ingredients, and the secret to a great batch version is the same as the secret to a great single pour: fresh lime juice, real agave, and a spirit with enough character to stand up to both. Moonshine brings a cleaner, sweeter base than tequila -- less agave funk, more corn smoothness -- and the triple sec bridges the two worlds.

The batch format is the key. You mix it all in a pitcher, taste it, adjust it, and stick it in the cooler. When people show up, all you have to do is salt the rims and pour. No shaking, no measuring, no bartending while the burgers burn. Just a pitcher, a stack of glasses, and a cooler full of ice.

This recipe scales easily. Hosting twenty? Triple it. Hosting two? Halve it. The ratios stay the same, and the result is always the same: the first pitcher is never enough.

Recipe

Batch Moonshine Margarita

Prep: 15 min
Serves: 8
Season: Summer

Ingredients

  • 2 cups moonshine
  • 1 cup lime juice (fresh squeezed)
  • 1 cup triple sec
  • ½ cup agave syrup
  • • Coarse salt for rim
  • • Lime wheels for garnish

Instructions

1

In a large pitcher, combine the moonshine, fresh lime juice, triple sec, and agave syrup. Stir vigorously until the agave is fully dissolved.

2

Taste and adjust. Add more agave if you prefer it sweeter, more lime if you like it tart. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

3

Run a lime wedge around the rim of each glass and dip in coarse salt. Fill with ice.

4

Pour the margarita mix over ice, garnish with a lime wheel, and serve.

Serve It In

Rocks glasses with salt rims for a classic look, or Montucky Mason Jars for a tailgate-friendly option that will not break.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far ahead can I make this? +
You can mix the full pitcher up to 24 hours ahead and keep it refrigerated. The lime juice will stay fresh overnight. Just give it a good stir before serving since the agave can settle.
Can I make it frozen? +
Yes -- pour the mixed batch into a blender with 4-5 cups of ice and blend until slushy. You may need to work in batches depending on your blender size. Add a little extra agave since freezing dulls sweetness.
What can I substitute for agave? +
Simple syrup (equal parts sugar and water) works perfectly. Honey syrup (2:1 honey to water) adds a richer, more complex sweetness that pairs well with the moonshine.
