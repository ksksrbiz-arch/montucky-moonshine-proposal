What to Mix with Moonshine: 7 Perfect Pairings

Simple mixers, proper ratios, and drinks worth making

Moonshine is one of the most versatile spirits you can pour. Its clean, corn-sweet character plays well with everything from citrus to spice, and its higher proof means it holds up in mixed drinks where lighter spirits would disappear. Whether you are new to moonshine or just looking for fresh ideas, these seven mixer categories will give you a starting point for some seriously good drinking.

A quick note on ratios: moonshine typically runs between 80 and 100 proof (40-50% ABV), which is stronger than most vodka or standard whiskey. Start with the ratios below and adjust to taste. You can always add more mixer; you cannot take it back.

1. Lemonade

Ratio: 1 part moonshine to 3 parts lemonade

This is the gateway moonshine drink, and for good reason. The tartness of the lemon cuts straight through moonshine's alcohol heat, while the sugar rounds out the corn sweetness into something dangerously drinkable. Use fresh-squeezed lemonade if you can -- the bottled stuff works fine, but fresh citrus makes a noticeable difference.

For an upgrade, muddle a few fresh mint leaves in the glass before adding ice. The mint adds a cooling note that makes this perfect for hot summer days. Serve in a mason jar for full effect.

Try it with: Plain corn moonshine or lemon-flavored moonshine for a double citrus hit.

2. Ginger Beer

Ratio: 1 part moonshine to 2-3 parts ginger beer

If the Moscow Mule works with vodka, imagine what happens when you swap in a spirit that actually has flavor. Ginger beer's spicy bite and effervescence complement moonshine's warmth beautifully. The carbonation lifts the drink and keeps it from feeling heavy, while the ginger adds a layer of complexity that plain soda cannot match.

Use a quality ginger beer with real ginger -- the spicier, the better. Add a squeeze of fresh lime and serve over plenty of ice in a copper mug if you have one. This is the moonshine equivalent of a cocktail bar staple, and it tastes like it costs twice what it does.

Try it with: Plain or apple-flavored moonshine. The apple-ginger combination is outstanding.

3. Apple Cider

Ratio: 1 part moonshine to 2-3 parts apple cider

This is a match made in Appalachian heaven. Apple and corn have been paired together since the earliest days of American farming, and there is a reason: the flavors are natural companions. The apple's sweetness and acidity balance the moonshine perfectly, and the resulting drink tastes like autumn in a glass.

Serve it cold over ice in the summer, or warm it gently on the stove in the fall and winter. Heated moonshine-and-cider with a cinnamon stick is one of the great cold-weather drinks. Just warm it -- do not boil it, or you will cook off the alcohol.

Try it with: Plain corn moonshine or apple pie moonshine. For the warm version, cinnamon-flavored moonshine is exceptional.

4. Sweet Tea

Ratio: 1 part moonshine to 3-4 parts sweet tea

Sweet tea and moonshine is one of those combinations that seems so obvious you wonder why it took anyone this long to think of it. The tannins in the tea provide a subtle bitterness that balances the sweetness, while the overall flavor is smooth, familiar, and incredibly easy to drink. This is a crowd-pleaser in the truest sense -- hand this to someone who has never had moonshine before and watch them become a convert.

Brew your tea strong and sweeten it while it is still hot so the sugar dissolves completely. Let it cool, then combine with moonshine over ice. A sprig of fresh mint and a lemon wedge turn this into something you could serve at any gathering.

Try it with: Plain corn moonshine or peach-flavored moonshine for the full Southern experience.

5. Cola

Ratio: 1 part moonshine to 2-3 parts cola

Whiskey and cola is one of the most popular mixed drinks on the planet, and moonshine-and-cola is its rougher, more interesting cousin. The caramel sweetness and carbonation of cola complement moonshine's corn character, and the result is a drink that is familiar enough to feel comfortable but distinctive enough to be worth talking about.

This works with any cola, but try it with a craft or Mexican cola made with real cane sugar. The cleaner sweetness of cane sugar lets more of the moonshine's flavor come through. Serve in a tall glass with plenty of ice and a lime wedge.

Try it with: Plain corn moonshine or cherry-flavored moonshine for an elevated cherry cola experience.

6. Fruit Juices

Ratio: 1 part moonshine to 2-3 parts juice

Moonshine's clean profile makes it an excellent base for fruit juice cocktails. Unlike heavily flavored spirits that compete with fruit, moonshine's neutral-leaning corn sweetness amplifies whatever juice you pair it with.

Best juice options:

  • Cranberry juice: The tartness is a perfect counterpoint. Add a splash of lime for a moonshine cosmopolitan.
  • Orange juice: Think of it as a moonshine screwdriver. Fresh-squeezed is best. Add a splash of grenadine for a moonshine sunrise.
  • Pineapple juice: Tropical and unexpected. The sweetness of pineapple and the sweetness of corn create a layered, complex drink.
  • Watermelon juice: Blend fresh watermelon and strain. Mixed with moonshine over ice, this is pure summer.
  • Peach nectar: Sweet, velvety, and Southern. Pairs especially well with peach-flavored moonshine for a double dose.

Try it with: Plain moonshine lets the fruit shine; flavored moonshine can create interesting double-flavor combinations.

7. Hot Chocolate

Ratio: 1 part moonshine to 3-4 parts hot chocolate

This is the dark horse on the list -- the combination you did not know you needed until you tried it. Rich, creamy hot chocolate with a shot of moonshine is extraordinary. The alcohol warms you from the inside while the chocolate provides a velvety sweetness that tames the moonshine's heat completely. It is like a hug in a mug.

Make your hot chocolate from scratch if you can -- real milk (or cream), good cocoa powder, and a touch of sugar. The richer the base, the better the drink. Top with whipped cream and a dusting of cinnamon or cocoa powder. This is a winter evening drink, meant for sitting by a fire and not going anywhere.

Try it with: Plain corn moonshine, vanilla-flavored moonshine, or -- for the adventurous -- cinnamon moonshine.

General Tips for Mixing Moonshine

  • Use plenty of ice. Moonshine's higher proof means dilution is your friend. As the ice melts, the drink opens up and the flavors integrate.
  • Start with less moonshine and add more. You can always make a drink stronger. You cannot make it weaker (without adding more mixer and making a bigger drink than you planned).
  • Fresh ingredients matter. Fresh citrus, fresh herbs, quality mixers -- they all make a noticeable difference.
  • Flavored moonshine is not cheating. If you have apple pie, peach, or cinnamon moonshine, lean into it. Use it with complementary mixers for layered, complex drinks.
  • Mason jars are not required. But they do make everything taste a little more authentic.
